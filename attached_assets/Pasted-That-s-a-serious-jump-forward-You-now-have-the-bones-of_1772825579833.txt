That’s a serious jump forward. You now have the bones of a real enterprise governance product, not just an MVP.

Based on the current state, I’d move next in this order:

## 1. Risk Assessment Wizard

This should be next.

Why:

* You already have registry, controls, workflows, evidence, audit logs, auth, and notifications.
* The biggest remaining leverage is improving the **quality of system intake**.
* A wizard reduces bad data entry, improves consistency, and makes the product easier for non-expert users.

What to include:

* step-by-step intake
* intended use
* affected users
* decision impact
* personal/sensitive data usage
* geography
* model/vendor
* human oversight questions
* risk outcome with explanation
* suggested controls/workflow based on answers

Important design choice:

* use a **deterministic rules engine first**
* store the explanation for every classification
* treat AI suggestions as optional later, not authoritative

That makes it defensible in audits.

## 2. Dashboard Trend Charts

Now they become meaningful.

Why now:

* you already have enough event data: systems, workflows, notifications, evidence, audit logs
* trend charts will now reflect real operational activity instead of static seed metrics

Best charts to add:

* systems by risk level over time
* approval throughput and aging
* overdue controls trend
* evidence attachment growth
* audit activity volume by week
* compliance completion by framework over time

This helps with exec demos and customer reporting.

## 3. Bulk Control Assignment

This is probably the next operational multiplier.

Why:

* once teams start onboarding many systems, control mapping becomes repetitive
* it will save time immediately for compliance teams

Good first version:

* multi-select systems
* assign one or more controls/framework mappings in batch
* preview impact before applying
* audit every bulk action
* permission-check every affected system

---

# After that

## 4. Activity Dashboard per User

This becomes strong now that you have:

* auth
* notifications
* workflows
* evidence tasks

Show each user:

* pending approvals
* unread notifications
* assigned controls
* overdue evidence requests
* recently touched systems

This improves daily engagement.

## 5. One integration connector

Do only one first.

Best first choices:

* **Jira** if your buyers are product/engineering-heavy
* **ServiceNow** if your buyers are enterprise risk/compliance-heavy

Reason:
one solid connector is far better than three shallow ones.

---

# What I would delay a bit longer

## AI-powered suggestions

Useful later, but now the main opportunity is to make the platform more structured and operationally efficient.

## Multi-tenant support

Only prioritize this if you are about to host multiple customers in one environment.

## Compliance calendar

Helpful, but lower impact than wizard, trends, and bulk assignment.

---

# Best roadmap from here

1. Risk Assessment Wizard
2. Dashboard Trend Charts
3. Bulk Control Assignment
4. Activity Dashboard per User
5. First external connector

That is the cleanest path from **enterprise-ready MVP** to **pilot-ready product with operational depth**.

The most important one is **Risk Assessment Wizard**. It improves data quality, user guidance, and compliance consistency all at once.
