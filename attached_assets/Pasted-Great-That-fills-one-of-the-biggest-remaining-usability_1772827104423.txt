## 1. First external connector

Pick **one** and do it deeply.

My recommendation:

* **Jira** first if your target users sit close to product, engineering, or platform teams
* **ServiceNow** first if you’re aiming at larger enterprise risk/compliance buyers

### Best first scope

* create a ticket from an approval workflow
* sync external status back into your workflow
* link ticket ID on system detail, workflow, and activity views
* optional rule: auto-create tickets for overdue controls or missing evidence

### Why this is next

You now have a strong internal operating model. The next commercial jump is showing that your platform fits into the tools companies already use.

---

## 2. Multi-tenant foundation

This should be next after the first connector, unless you’re about to onboard multiple customers into one shared deployment right now.

### Why now

Your product has enough depth that multiple orgs could use it. Delaying tenancy too long makes later retrofitting painful across:

* users
* notifications
* evidence
* exports
* dashboards
* integrations

### What to implement

* organization table
* tenant_id on every business entity
* tenant-scoped auth and RBAC
* tenant-safe file storage paths
* tenant-safe exports and queries
* middleware/enforcement layer so tenant filtering is automatic, not manual

---

## 3. Compliance Calendar

This becomes much more useful now that you have personal dashboards, workflows, evidence, and due dates.

### Include

* internal review deadlines
* evidence due dates
* overdue controls
* reassessment reminders
* EU AI Act milestone dates
* workflow deadlines by owner/reviewer

This is a strong feature for compliance leads and auditors.
